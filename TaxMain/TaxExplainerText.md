Key Tax Credit Eligibility (2025/2026)

* Basic Personal Tax Credit: Available to all taxpayers. €2,000 for single, €4,000 for married/civil partners.
* Employee (PAYE) Tax Credit: Available to individuals receiving income taxed under PAYE, including salary and pensions (€2,000).
* Rent Tax Credit: Available to tenants paying rent on a registered tenancy with the Residential Tenancies Board (RTB).
* Single Person Child Carer Credit: For single parents caring for a dependent child.
* Home Carer Tax Credit: For married couples where one spouse cares for a dependent person at home.
* Age Tax Credit: Available for people aged 65 or over.
* Medical/Dental Expenses: Tax relief of 20% on qualifying costs, such as GP visits, prescriptions, and non-routine dental care.
* Dependent Relative Tax Credit: Available to individuals who financially support a dependent relative (€305)

Key 2026 Income Tax Details

* Standard Rate Band: 20% on the first €44,000 for single/widowed individuals, or up to €48,000 for one-parent families.
* Higher Rate Band: 40% on all earnings exceeding the standard rate cut-off point.
* Married Couples/Civil Partners: The standard rate band can be increased up to a maximum of €53,000 (if one spouse has income) or up to a maximum of €88,000 (if both partners have income).
* Tax Credits:
* &#x20;These are subtracted from your total tax due to reduce the final amount paid. Key credits include Personal Tax Credit and Employee Tax Credit (PAYE credit).
* PAYE (Pay As You Earn): The system used to deduct income tax directly from your earnings by your employer.
* USC (Universal Social Charge): A separate tax charged on gross income, generally applying if income exceeds €13,000 per year, with varying rates.
* PRSI (Pay Related Social Insurance): Paid to the Department of Social Protection to fund social welfare benefits.



Tax bands:

* Single / Widowed (without children): 20% on the first €44,000; 40% on the balance.
* Married (One Income): 20% on the first €53,000; 40% on the balance.
* Married (Two Incomes): 20% on up to €88,000 (max €44,000 each) and 40% on the balance.
* Single Parent / Widowed Parent: 20% on the first €48,000; 40% on the balance. 



PRSI

Pay Related Social Insurance (PRSI) in Ireland is a compulsory contribution based on earnings, paid by employees and employers to fund social welfare benefits.

USC

In Ireland, USC stands for Universal Social Charge, a tax payable on gross income, including notional pay (benefits), introduced in 2011. It applies to individuals with a total income over €13,000 per year, with rates applied on a sliding scale to the total, not just the amount over the threshold.
